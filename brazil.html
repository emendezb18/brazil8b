Amazon rainforest

The amazon rainforest covers an area of 6,000,000 square km, the amazon is the world’s richest most varied biological reservoir, containing millions of species of insects, plants, birds, people (24 million) and more than 180 indigenous groups, also other forms of life. It has a lot of different species of trees like for example myrtle, laurel, palm, acacia, rose wood, brazil nut, and rubber tree, its major animal species are the jaguar, the manatee, the tapir, the red deer and the capybara. In addition to its diversity it also is a center of control on the plants climate, the amazon basin stores approximately 100 billion metric tons of carbon, which is more than ten times the annual global emission from fossil fuels. Around 15 million years ago a massive freshwater lake covered the amazon basin, but during the several ice ages, water started to flow to the east from the andes, sea level fell, the lake started to drain out to the ocean, and then the amazon river was created. The rainforest is important to Brazil because it contributes about 8.2 billion a year to Brazil's economy economy, from products including rubber and wood. The rainforest brings rainfall across south america and much of the continent would become hotter and dryer if the rainforest was to be destroyed. Tourists can visit the amazon rainforest, but it is important to visit in an ethical way. Which means going with a tour or a well trained guide. Guides will help you visit and experience the amazon rainforest at its fullest potential while affirming that you visit morally and not damaging to the area or the wildlife.

Christ the redemeer

Christ the Redeemer, which is Cristo redentor in Portuguese, is the statue of Jesus Christ at the summit of Mount Corcovado, it is 30 meters tall and it is made of reinforced concrete. It was made in 1921 when the Roman catholic Archdiocese of Rio de Janeiro proposed a statue of Christ to be built on a summit so that everyone in Rio could see it. The statue has become a symbolic protection for people, like Jesus, the statue protects the urban environment especially for christians. People believe it provides protection for the soul and that it's as important as any shelter. The most famous traditional way of visiting Christ the redeemer is by cog train. The train will take you to the top of the atlantic forest so that you can watch the statue from every angle in only 30 minutes!

Rio Carnival Week:

Entitled as “ the greatest show on earth ” , the Rio Carnival never fails to give anyone an impressive fun party. It can take up to a year to plan, because of how many details there are to it. All the performances are prepared by the best 12 samba schools in Brazil. The choreography and music can last up to 75 minutes each one with a different theme. Although the practice of Carnivals originated from Europe, African influence is evident in today's Brazil's carnivals. This began when Brazil became a colony of Portugal. The carnivals were made by adapting tribal dances which included parading around the village which was done to protect themselves from bad spirits in the area. Then people began to use costumes and tribal masks during the celebrations. It's really important for Brazil, because it's been an important tradition for many years on repeat and it also has a religious spiritual meaning for them. It's one of the biggest attractions in the world. The stadium can seat more than 80,000 people. Their first need is to build a facility for the comfort of tourists and for locals. Before Sambodromo existed, the top samba schools did the parade on the streets of Rio, but this all changed when Rio officials and organizers said that they needed change for more order. The change was made in 1984. After making Sombodromo there was a huge increase of participants and spectators.

Museum of tomorrow

The museum of tomorrow is a science museum in Rio de Janeiro.The visionary museum is focused on answering five questions : Where did we come from ? Who are we ? Where are we ? Where are we going ? And, how do we want to live together in fifty years ? The Museum’s exhibits will address issues including population growth and increased life expectancy, consumption patterns, climate change, genetic engineering and bioethics, the distribution of wealth, technological advances and changes in biodiversity. It was designed by the spanish architect : Santiago Calatrava, and it was built next to beach at Pier Maua. The building was opened on december 17 of 2015 with the company of president Dilma Rouseff. It can be really important because its an arts and science museum wich makes it more educational, some examples are that it exhibits warning of the dangers of climate change, environmental degradation, and social collapse. A lot of tourists decide to visit for educational popouses but also to see the beautiful inside art designs.

Iguaçu Falls:

Iguazu falls are waterfalls located on the border in Argentina and Brazil, their height is 82 meters and their width is 2700 meters. The name Iguazu comes from tupi words (ancient indigenous people) meaning water , uasu, and big Legend has it that a deity planned to marry a beautiful woman named Naipí, who fled with her mortal lover Tarobá in a canoe. In a rage, the deity sliced the river, creating the waterfalls and condemning the lovers to an eternal fall. The first European to record the existence of the falls was the Spanish Conquistador Álvar Núñez Cabeza de Vaca in 1541. They can be really important because lots of people love visiting important bodies of water and it also holds an interesting stoy that some people could believe in. The Iguazu falls usually receive more than 1 million tourists per year.There are two options to meet the Iguazu Falls: the Brazilian side or the Argentinean side. They are separate tours, and for each one you will have to pay a ticket, but worth the price, since they are experimenting with different perspectives. The access to Iguazu Falls in Argentina, is done through the Argentinean Iguazú National Park, in this case it is necessary to enter the country through the customs, being essentially your personal documents and passport. On the Brazilian side, the tour lasts 2-4 hours, starting with the bus ride, during this route there are stops for additional sightseeing tours (not included in the value of the tour). The trail is performed on foot by a way in the woods, with open spaces for observation of the Iguazu Falls.
